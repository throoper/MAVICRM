  2. Talent                                                                                                                                                                                                                         - Talent appears as a key entity — can you explain what "talent" means in your business context? Are these workers/contractors that you place with client companies?                                                              
- Is talent associated with specific companies/contracts, or managed separately?       

Answers:
- We are a talent marketplace where we connect global accounting with clients in the US that need experienced accountants. These are contractors that we place to fill part time or full time positions with a client's company. Once we place the individual we have no insight into their work apart from the hours they log and what they input into their time tracking software. Work quality and deliverables are all managed by the client. 
- Talent is managed separately but for the purposes of the CRM Talent are also attached to a contract for a client. Our contracts include the client and the talent in question. It is possible for one client to have multiple contracts, if they filled multiple roles, with different talent. It is also possible for talent to be on multiple contracts with multiple companies, if they are being used in multiple part time engagements for example.

  - Who creates issues — internal team only, or can clients report them?                                                                                                                                                           
 - What fields does an issue have? (severity, category, assigned owner, etc.)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      
  
Answers:
- I would say Open > In Progress > Monitoring Solution > Resolved - sometimes we have to replace talent that is underperforming for example. In this scenario when we put the new person in we would have our solution in place but want to ensure it receives more attention for a few weeks before we mark it as resolved. 
- All issues would be created by the internal team at this moment after a report from client, talent, or internal metrics. I'd like to have full client issue reporting but that is not part of this MVP.
